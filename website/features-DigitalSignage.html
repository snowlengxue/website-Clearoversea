Q & A

What is digital signage and what is its purpose?

Digital Signage refers to a variety of technologies used to reduce our reliance on traditional paper-based signage on campus. Rather than using static print signage and bulletin boards, digital signage is composed of electronic displays that disperse information in a timely and targeted manner.

Where is the Digital Signage Program available?

Carleton’s digital signage program was launched in the fall of 2011 in a variety of campus locations, including Sayles-Hill Great Space, Sayles-Hill (outside the Student Activities Office), Burton Dining Hall (2 locations), East Dining Hall, Office of Admissions, Gould Library, Recreation Center and the Arts Union (2 locations). Additional locations may be added in the future.

Who may submit advertisements, announcements, etc. to the digital signage program?

All submissions must come from a current member of the Carleton community (i.e., faculty, staff, or student) and be relevant to a Carleton service, event or program.

What may be submitted?

  • One advertisement relevant to an upcoming Carleton event (e.g., “Fridays with Fred”, “Sayles Dance Saturday”, “Movie tonight in the Arts Union”)
  • Services available to students (e.g., LOCAL bus schedule, commencement information, Post Office hours)
  • Personal Kudos (e.g., “Happy Birthday”, “Best of Luck at the Tournament”, “Congratulations on Comps”). Please note: Personal kudos will only display on the dining hall monitors.

What is not permitted?

The following are examples of advertisements that are not permitted due to technical issues or because they conflict with Carleton’s Community Standards and Policies. The Digital Signage Program reserves the right to edit or deny advertisements for any reason.

  • Classified ads (lost & found, ride share, for sale, etc.) should be placed in the NNB
  • Advertisements with video or sound are currently not supported by the technology
  • Copyrighted/trademarked material not owned by the person submitting the advertisement
  • Advertisements that promote the use of alcohol or drugs

How do I submit to the Digital Signage Program?

Please see the Publishing on the Signs page for details.

What is the submission deadline?

Ideally, advertisements should be submitted 48 hours (business days) in advance. Often times requests will be processed sooner, but please use this as a guideline. Also keep in mind that space is limited and changes may be necessary. Start the process early!

Am I required to include graphics in my advertisement or can I submit text only?

In order to keep the displays interesting, advertisements should not be text only.

How many days will my advertisement run and how frequently will it be displayed?

Only one advertisement per event or service may be submitted. Date-specific advertisements will automatically be removed at the completion of the event and typically run for a maximum of 7 days. Non-date specific advertisements may be selected to run more frequently. A single advertisement may not be submitted for sequential weeks — please choose the single week during which your ad will be most effective.

How many advertisements will run each day?

A maximum of 30 advertisements can run per day, each preset with a 7 second display time. These 30 slots are on a first come, first served basis.

Is there a charge to place an advertisement?

No, this is a free service to the Carleton community.

How do I cancel or change an ad?

Please contact Student Activities to cancel an ad you have submitted.

What happens to the monitors during a campus emergency?

Carleton College has teamed up with e2campus to provide time sensitive information to the campus community. During an emergency all monitors will display updated information and instructions. This service will only be used in the event of life-threatening situations affecting the entire campus; e.g. tornado warnings, major chemical spills, threats of armed violence, etc.
